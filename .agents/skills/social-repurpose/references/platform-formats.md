## LinkedIn

### Constraints
| Field | Limit |
|---|---|
| Post text | 3,000 characters max (aim for 900-1,300) |
| "See more" fold | ~210 characters before truncation |
| Hashtags | 3-5 per post (more hurts reach) |
| Images | 1200x627px recommended for link previews |
| Video | Up to 10 minutes, native upload preferred |

### Formatting Rules
- **Line breaks matter** â€” LinkedIn rewards white space. Use single blank lines between paragraphs.
- **Hook line is critical** â€” First 2 lines appear before "See more." Make them count.
- **No markdown** â€” LinkedIn doesn't render markdown. Use Unicode characters for emphasis if needed:
  - Bold: surround text with asterisks (LinkedIn renders **bold**)
  - Bullets: use `â†’` or `â€¢` characters
- **Hashtags at the end** â€” Place hashtags on the last line, separated by spaces.
- **No links in the first comment** â€” Some strategies move links to the first comment to avoid algorithm penalties. Default: include link in the post body.

### Best Practices
- Posts with 1,200-1,500 characters get 2x more engagement than short posts
- Ask a question to drive comments (comments boost reach more than likes)
- Personal stories outperform corporate announcements
- First-person perspective ("I learned..." not "This article explains...")
- Avoid: "I'm excited to announce," "Thrilled to share," "Check out my latest"

### Hook Formulas
1. **Contrarian:** "Everyone says [common belief]. They're wrong. Here's why:"
2. **Data-driven:** "[Specific stat] changed how I think about [topic]."
3. **Story:** "Last week, [something happened]. It taught me [lesson]."
4. **Question:** "What would you do if [scenario]?"
5. **List tease:** "After [experience], here are [N] things I wish I'd known:"

## Twitter/X

### Constraints
| Field | Limit |
|---|---|
| Tweet | 280 characters max |
| Thread | No limit on tweets, but 3-7 is optimal |
| Hashtags | 1-2 per tweet max (more hurts engagement) |
| Links | Count as 23 characters regardless of length |
| Images | Up to 4 per tweet, 5MB max each |
| Polls | Up to 4 options, 25 characters each |

### Formatting Rules
- **Thread numbering:** Use `1/` or `1/7` format at the start of each tweet
- **No tweet should depend on the previous** â€” Each should make sense standalone (people see individual tweets in feeds)
- **Hook tweet has no number** â€” Some strategies skip numbering on tweet 1 to make it look like a standalone post
- **Final tweet:** CTA + link + optional hashtags
- **Whitespace:** Use line breaks within tweets for readability

### Best Practices
- Hook tweet gets 80% of impressions â€” spend most effort there
- Threads with 5-7 tweets get highest engagement
- Use specific numbers over vague claims ("increased revenue 34%" not "grew revenue significantly")
- Quote-tweet your own hook tweet with a summary to re-share the thread
- Reply to your thread with the link (keeps the hook tweet clean)
- Avoid: threads that could be a single tweet, excessive self-promotion

### Hook Formulas
1. **Bold claim:** "[Specific outcome] in [timeframe]. Here's the exact playbook:"
2. **List tease:** "[N] things about [topic] that most people get wrong:"
3. **Observation:** "I've noticed [pattern]. Here's what's actually happening:"
4. **Challenge:** "Hot take: [contrarian opinion about common practice]"
5. **Before/after:** "[Thing] was broken. Then we [change]. Results:"

## Instagram

### Constraints
| Field | Limit |
|---|---|
| Caption | 2,200 characters max |
| "More" fold | ~125 characters before truncation |
| Hashtags | 30 max per post (20-30 recommended) |
| Bio link | One link (use link-in-bio service for multiple) |
| Carousel | Up to 20 slides |

### Formatting Rules
- **Line breaks:** Instagram collapses multiple line breaks. Use a period or invisible character on empty lines to force breaks.
- **Hashtag block:** Separate hashtags from caption with 5 line breaks (dots or invisible chars). This pushes them below the fold.
- **Emojis:** Use 2-4 per paragraph. Place them at the start of bullet points or as section dividers.
- **No clickable links** â€” Links in captions are not clickable. Use "Link in bio" CTA.
- **Mentions:** Tag relevant accounts with @handle.

### Best Practices
- First line must hook â€” it appears in the feed before "...more"
- Carousel posts get 1.4x more reach than single images
- Save-worthy content (tips, frameworks, data) performs best
- Ask a question at the end to drive comments
- Mix hashtag sizes: 10 broad (500K+ posts), 10 medium (50K-500K), 10 niche (<50K)
- Post hashtags in the caption, not the first comment (algorithm change in 2024)

### Hashtag Strategy
Build three tiers:
1. **Broad (10):** High-volume, general topic hashtags (#marketing, #business, #entrepreneur)
2. **Medium (10):** Category-specific hashtags (#saasmarketing, #pricingstrategy, #b2bsales)
3. **Niche (10):** Highly targeted hashtags (#saasfounder, #pricingtips, #revenueoptimization)

## Facebook

### Constraints
| Field | Limit |
|---|---|
| Post text | 63,206 characters max |
| "See more" fold | ~477 characters before truncation |
| Hashtags | 0-2 max (hashtags hurt Facebook reach) |
| Link preview | Auto-generated from URL (title, description, image) |
| Video | Up to 240 minutes |

### Formatting Rules
- **Front-load value** â€” The first 477 characters appear before "See more." Put the hook and key insight there.
- **Link on its own line** â€” Place the article URL on its own line at the end for a clean link preview.
- **Minimal formatting** â€” Facebook doesn't support markdown or special characters well. Keep it conversational.
- **No hashtags** â€” Facebook's algorithm deprioritizes posts with hashtags. Skip them or use 1-2 max.

### Best Practices
- Questions drive 2x more comments than statements
- Native video gets 10x more reach than link posts
- Posts with 40-80 characters get highest engagement, but longer posts work for articles
- Facebook rewards posts that start conversations â€” end with a discussion prompt
- Tag relevant pages/people when genuine (not spam tagging)
- Avoid: excessive emojis, hashtag strings, "engagement bait" (Facebook penalizes "comment YES if...")

### Hook Formulas
1. **Question:** "Have you ever [common experience]? Here's what most people get wrong:"
2. **Surprising stat:** "We found that [counterintuitive data point]. Here's what that means:"
3. **Relatable frustration:** "You know that feeling when [pain point]? There's actually a fix."
4. **Quick tip:** "Quick tip for [audience]: [one-sentence insight]. Here's the full story:"
5. **Story opener:** "Last month we [did something]. The results surprised us."

## Cross-Platform Guidelines

### Differentiation Rules
Each platform post should feel **native**, not reformatted. Key differences:

| Aspect | LinkedIn | Twitter/X | Instagram | Facebook |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tone | Professional, insightful | Direct, punchy | Casual, visual | Conversational, personal |
| Length | Long (900-1,300 chars) | Short (280/tweet) | Medium (1,000-1,500) | Medium (500-1,000) |
| Hashtags | 3-5 | 1-2 in final tweet | 20-30 in block | 0-2 |
| Emojis | Minimal (0-2) | Moderate (1-2/tweet) | Frequent (2-4/para) | Minimal (1-2) |
| CTA style | Comment/discuss | Follow/RT | Link in bio | Click link/comment |
| Hook style | Insight/story | Bold claim/data | Visual/emotional | Question/story |
| Links | In post body | In final tweet | "Link in bio" | Own line at end |

### Content Adaptation (Not Duplication)
- **Same core message** across all platforms
- **Different framing** for each audience
- **Different hooks** â€” don't use the same opening line across platforms
- **Different CTAs** â€” match platform behavior (LinkedIn: discuss, Twitter: retweet, Instagram: save, Facebook: click)
- **Different depth** â€” LinkedIn goes deep, Twitter distills, Instagram visualizes, Facebook discusses
